Bio

The cherry blossom lined street of Mt. Royal Ave taught me to experience my surroundings during my five years at the Maryland Ins., College of Art. My professors taught me to take a different direction to work every day and not get stuck in my head in anything I did. I graduated in 2001.

My BFA from M.I.C.A. landed me my job today as a creative assistant for news anchor Melanie Lawson and CEO of HMAAC John Guess. Prior to that, an assortment of service industry jobs and freelance art projects led me to open an art store called Artbinsters mid pandemic. I managed the store for a year and a half until closing it in March of 2022. Artbinsters filled a giant tool box with experience for me.

Since college, I’ve lived in Baltimore, Houston, Las Vegas, Nampa Idaho, Monterey California, San Jose, San Francisco, Redwood City, San Diego ( during which I took a front end web development class at S.D.C.C.) and Waco Texas. Of many experiences, I’ve walked four miles to walk four more inside the Louvre, swam with whale sharks off Tulum, saw a sea turtle lay eggs, given art tours at Burning Man, and sky dived in Monterey. Ten years after leaving Houston, I returned home to Texas in April of 2016.

Art projects consist of many paintings, murals, photography, book design, video projects, you tube channel, etc. One of the grandest, replicating Sandro Botticellis The Birth of Venus and La Primavera at Sorrentos Restaurant in 2005.

Today I look to the future to work on projects that utilize my broad skill set to create content that connects and helps people in every nook and cranny of the world.
